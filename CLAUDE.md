Brand Vision & North Star

HESSENTIALS

1. Hessentials — Brand Manifesto

Hessentials exists because modern life is loud, cluttered, and optimized for speed—not meaning.

We are surrounded by advice, products, content, and “aesthetic” inspiration, yet most of it feels hollow, rushed, or interchangeable. Pinterest-perfect but emotionally vacant. Useful, maybe—but not grounding.

Hessentials is a response to that.

This brand is about intentional living without pretension. About choosing well instead of choosing more. About building a life that feels edited, warm, and human—one meal, one room, one ritual at a time.

Hessentials does not chase trends. It distills them.
It does not perform luxury. It defines quality.
It does not sell perfection. It sells confidence in your choices.

Everything under the Hessentials umbrella—food, home, hosting, style, design, routines—is rooted in the same belief:
a well-considered life is a calmer, more powerful one.

Hessentials is not aspirational fantasy.
It’s real life—made thoughtful.

2. Hessentials — One-Sentence Mission

Hessentials helps people live more intentionally by curating and creating essentials that make everyday life feel grounded, elevated, and deeply personal.

(Investor note: this scales—from content to product to experience—without boxing you in.)

3. Hessentials — What We Are / What We Are Not

What We Are

A curated lifestyle brand, not a catch-all influencer platform
Rooted in taste, discernment, and lived experience
Practical and emotional—form always meets feeling
Designed for people who value quality but reject excess
Built slowly, intentionally, and with longevity in mind

What We Are Not

Not trend-chasing or algorithm-driven
Not mass-market lifestyle noise
Not sterile minimalism or over-designed luxury
Not performative wellness or aspirational cosplay
Not a Pinterest board without a point of view

If it doesn’t feel personal, useful, or timeless—it doesn’t belong.

4. Hessentials — Founder POV (Jordan Hess)

Hessentials is an extension of how I already live, think, and decide.

I’ve spent my career helping brands clarify who they are, what matters, and how to communicate that clearly. Over time, I realized I was applying that same discipline to my own life—editing instead of accumulating, choosing pieces and practices that felt intentional rather than impressive.

Hessentials is my way of sharing that lens.

I’m not interested in telling people how to live. I’m interested in showing what it looks like to choose well—to cook meals that feel grounding, design spaces that feel calm, host people with ease, and build routines that support real life.

This brand exists at the intersection of taste, utility, and emotional intelligence.
It’s warm, not precious. Elevated, not inaccessible. Confident, not loud.

Hessentials isn’t about having everything.
It’s about knowing what matters—and standing by it.

# 📌 Primary Audience Profile

**Hessentials — v1**

### Working Name

**The Intentional Host**

(We can rename later. This is a placeholder, not branding.)

---

## Who They Are

**Demographics**

* Age: **28–45**
* Location: **Urban & inner-suburban markets** (Houston, Austin, Dallas, Atlanta, LA, NYC, Chicago)
* Income: **$120k+ household**, discretionary spending intact
* Education: College-educated; culturally literate
* Household: Single, partnered, or married; kids optional but not central

---

## How They See Themselves

They care deeply about:

* taste
* presentation
* atmosphere
* being “the one who has it together” socially

They are:

* not flashy
* not maximalist
* allergic to gimmicks
* drawn to *quiet confidence*

They want their life to feel:

* intentional
* elevated
* calm but impressive

They value:

* quality over quantity
* curation over chaos
* guidance over endless choice

---

## Their Core Problem

They want to host, cook, and live beautifully **without it feeling like a second job**.

They are overwhelmed by:

* too many recipes
* too many products
* too many opinions
* lifestyle content that is either unrealistic or soulless

They don’t need inspiration.
They need **editing**.

---

## What They’re Already Spending Money On

* Premium groceries (Whole Foods, Central Market, local markets)
* Cookbooks they admire but don’t fully use
* Home items that *almost* work together
* Hosting supplies bought last-minute
* Subscriptions, planners, and “systems” they abandon

📌 *Key insight:*
They are already paying for taste—but not for **clarity**.

---

## What They Want (But Can’t Articulate Yet)

They want someone to say:

> “Here’s the right way to do this. Trust me.”

They want:

* fewer decisions
* better outcomes
* confidence in execution
* permission to stop overthinking

They are not looking for trends.
They are looking for *judgment* they trust.

---

## Why They Will Pay Hessentials First

They will pay because Hessentials:

* saves time **and** mental energy
* makes them look effortlessly put-together
* validates their instinct for quality
* gives them repeatable wins (not one-offs)

They don’t want a one-time recipe.
They want a **point of view** they can return to.

---

## What Success Looks Like for Them

* Hosting feels calm, not frantic
* Their home feels cohesive
* Their food feels intentional
* Their friends assume “this just comes naturally to them”

They don’t want praise.
They want *recognition*.

---

## What Hessentials Is to Them

Not a brand.
Not a blog.
Not a personality.

Hessentials is:

* a trusted editor
* a quiet authority
* a shortcut to good taste
* a system for living well without excess

---

## Hard Boundary (Important)

This audience is **not**:

* bargain-first
* trend-chasing
* interested in hacks
* impressed by volume or virality

If it doesn’t feel considered, they’re out.

### Why This Is the Right Primary Audience

* They **value guidance**
* They **become repeat buyers**

This audience builds businesses.

## TYPOGRAPHY — DASHES

Em dashes (—) in body copy and eyebrows: use surrounding spaces.

```
word — word
PILLAR — SUBCATEGORY
```

En dashes (–) in numeric and date ranges: no surrounding spaces.

```
$55–$75
April 27–May 3, 2026
```

Hyphens (-): only for compound words.

```
long-form
10-minute
five-piece
```

Never substitute one for another.
