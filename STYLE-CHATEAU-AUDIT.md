# STYLE — Chateau Audit

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Scope:** All 14 articles in `src/data/style.ts`
**Filter:** Chateau ghostwriter spec (STYLE only)

---

## Headline Finding

The voice is right. The substance isn't built yet.

No banned phrases appear anywhere in the file. The first three articles read like Jordan wrote them. The remaining eleven read like outlines waiting to be filled in — correct framework, correct tone where there's tone at all, but the body sections are stubs. Most have one-line essays and Shop sections with a single product, which is not a Shop section. It's a footnote.

The fix is not a rewrite of the system. It's filling in articles 4–14 to the depth of articles 1–3.

## Per-Article Verdicts

### 01. The Uniform Is Not Boring — **PASS**
Strongest piece in the set. Use it as the reference template for everything else.
- Opening states a specific exhaustion immediately. No scene-setting.
- "thank God, I already know what works" — sounds like Jordan, not a stylist.
- "personal rebrand" and "If your outfit cannot survive a last-minute dinner plan" earn their lines.
- Callout is sharp. Practical is actionable. Shop has four real items with real reasons.
- **Action:** Leave it.

### 02. Elevated Casual Is a Discipline — **PASS**
- Subtitle works as a reframe: "Effortless is built. Not accidental."
- "a real pant instead of something that gave up at the waistband" is the kind of line Chateau exists to protect.
- Callout is sharp.
- **Note:** "elevated" appears in title, intro, essay, and across multiple other pieces. Not banned, but it's becoming a tic across the set. Watch the frequency.
- **Action:** Leave it. Maybe trade one "elevated" for "sharper" or "intentional."

### 03. Texture Is the Outfit — **PASS**
- "I will always care more about texture than color" — clear opinion, stated cleanly.
- "something loud that does not know what it is doing" — good.
- The cross-domain pull (clothes, homes, tables, food) earns the brand's reach.
- **Action:** Leave it.

### 04. The 5-Piece Rule — **LIGHT EDIT**
- Body is clean but the article promises five parts (base, structure, texture, contrast, finish) and the Shop section delivers four. Either add the fifth piece or rename the framework.
- Essay could spend two more lines making each of the five concrete instead of just listing.
- **Action:** Add a fifth Shop item. Expand the essay by ~3 sentences so each of the five gets a single sharp definition.

### 05. The Details That Change Everything — **LIGHT EDIT**
- Opening is sharp ("No one compliments 'good sleeve length.'").
- Body is brutally short. That can work — but here it doesn't quite earn the page.
- **Action:** Add 2–3 specifics to the essay (cuff width on a knit, how a half-break reads vs. a full break, why suede shows fingerprints). The Shop section needs at least one more item — a shoe brush or a tailor recommendation.

### 06. The Anti-Trend Rule — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- Premise is right; execution is a stub.
- Intro is one sentence. Essay is three short fragments. Practical is three items. Shop is one item.
- The page reads as if the file was saved before it was finished.
- **Action:** Build it out. Give the reader a real test for distinguishing borrowed-from-trend vs. owned-by-you. One worked example. A second Shop item.

### 07. The Signature Piece — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- Intro fails the "no broad claims" rule: "This is where style becomes identity" is a wrap-up disguised as an opening.
- Essay's "A jacket, a shoe, a watch, a scent. Not ten. One." is the only real line.
- One Shop item.
- **Action:** Open with a specific person (real or composite) recognizable for one piece. Build essay to explain how to *find* the piece, not just commit. Three Shop items, one per category.

### 08. The Entryway Test — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- The "test" promised in the title is never defined. That's the article.
- Intro ("People decide how they feel about your space immediately") is generic and could appear in any home blog.
- "Your entry needs a lamp before anything else" is the only line worth keeping as-is.
- **Action:** Define the test in 2–3 criteria. Walk through what passes and what fails with concrete details. Add at least one more Shop item — a hook, a tray, a mirror.

### 09. Signature Scent Is Branding — **LIGHT EDIT, FLAG TITLE**
- Body voice is good. "Recognition over volume. It should sit close, not announce itself" is correct Chateau.
- Callout is sharp.
- **Title flag:** "Branding" is the kind of corporate-speak the spec warns against — "Should not feel like brand voice." Even as ironic framing, it reads slightly off. Consider: *Your Scent Is Your Signature*, *The Scent People Remember*, or *One Scent, Worn Often.*
- Body is thin: intro is one line, essay is one line.
- **Action:** Rename. Expand essay by 2–3 sentences (close vs. projecting, day vs. evening, why people misread loud as confident). One additional Shop item at a lower price point.

### 10. Your Home Has an Outfit Too — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- Concept is one of the strongest in the brand. Execution is the thinnest in the file.
- Essay is literally one line: "Base, structure, texture, finish."
- "You already know how to do this" is a strong opener but isolated.
- **Action:** Translate each of the four (base = sofa or rug, structure = case goods, texture = textiles, finish = lighting/objects) with one concrete example each. Three Shop items.

### 11. Hosting Is a Performance — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- Hosting is core to the audience ("The Intentional Host"). This article should be one of the strongest. It's one of the thinnest.
- Essay: "Light, sound, pacing." Three nouns, no body.
- "Overhead lighting ruins everything" is the right kind of line. There should be eight more.
- **Action:** Build out each of the three (light, sound, pacing) into its own move. Add a 4th section on what *not* to do (welcome speeches, plated apps, asking what people want to drink). At least three Shop items: candle, speaker, glassware.

### 12. The Dinner Plate Is a Style Object — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- "It is the point" is a strong response, but to a question the article never asks.
- Essay is "Space, color, texture." — same pattern as #10 and #11.
- **Action:** Open by naming the mistake (overcrowded plate, edge-to-edge). Define the three with examples. Add an item: serving piece or linen napkin.

### 13. Style Is Not Gendered — **KILL SWITCH**
- This article fails the authenticity filter. "Is this useful? Does this avoid sounding like content?" — neither.
- Total body content: "Fit, proportion, texture." / "Different expression." / "Good taste translates."
- The position is fine. The execution reads as a placeholder, and shipping it at this length feels performative — the opposite of the spec's intent.
- **Action:** Either rewrite from scratch with real substance (a worked example of one piece worn three ways across different identities, a specific argument for why fit reads first), or pull it from the live set until it's ready. As-is, it weakens the category.

### 14. The Bag Sets the Tone — **HEAVY REWRITE**
- "Before you even speak" is a good fragment. The rest is one-liners.
- Callout is sharp.
- **Action:** Build essay around the difference between a bag that holds your day and a bag that becomes the day's first problem. Add specifics: structure vs. slouch, leather vs. canvas, one bag vs. rotation. Two more Shop items at different price tiers.

## Patterns Across the Set

**The voice is solid.** No banned phrases. No "additionally / furthermore / in conclusion." No "must-haves." No "curated closets." The Chateau filter held on the lines that exist.

**The substance is not.** Eleven of fourteen articles read as scaffolding. The pattern is consistent: 1–2 line intro, 1–3 line essay, 3–4 line callout, 3–4 practical bullets, 1 Shop item. That's not a Chateau article — that's the outline before one.

**Shop sections need a minimum.** A Shop section with one item is not a section. Establish a floor: 3 items minimum. Different price tiers when possible. Each with a real reason and a real alt.

**Watch "elevated."** It's not banned, but it appears in 4+ places across the set. Build a small rotation: *sharper, intentional, considered, grounded.*

**Some intros are reflections, not openings.** "This is where style becomes identity," "First impressions are fast," "Scent is memory," "It is the same instinct" — these are the kind of broad claims the spec says to avoid. The opener should state a tension or a truth specific to *this* article.

## Recommendation

Don't rewrite. **Build out.** The voice is correct. The framework is correct. What's missing is the second draft — the one where Jordan went back through and added the lines that make each section earn its place.

Start with Hosting. Calibrate the depth there, then propagate the same standard across the queue.
