A grown sneaker is rare. Most try too hard — chunky soles, performance branding, technical fabric panels meant to suggest movement. The court silhouette is the opposite move. Low profile, leather upper, no bubble underfoot, no story being told.

## What it does for the look

It works under tailoring. A linen pant doesn't fight a clean court sneaker the way it fights a runner. Cropped wool trousers sit on top of the shoe instead of being interrupted by it. The shoe holds the line of the leg.

## What you actually get

A sneaker that doesn't age out. The court shape predates streetwear and will outlast it. Leather softens with wear, holds shape, and patinas in a way fabric panels never will. Prada's finish is the part you're paying for — the construction is tighter than the volume players at this price tier.

## What to wear it with

Linen pants. Cotton chinos. A clean wool trouser if you want to push it. Not with shorts — the silhouette stops working below the calf. Not with formal denim if you're past 35; the combination starts reading like a uniform from a different decade.

The point of a clean sneaker is that it disappears. This one does, in a way that makes the rest of the outfit look more deliberate.
