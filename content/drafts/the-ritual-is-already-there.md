You don't need a new routine.

You don't need to add anything.

The ritual is already there.

The morning. The meal. The table.

We are only asking you to notice it.

---

## The Morning

Before the day starts, there is a quieter version of it.

The kettle. The window. The first cup.

This hour is not new.

It has been yours every day of your life.

---

The shift is not what you do.

It is whether you stay in it.

---

A ritual is not what you add to the morning.

It is what the morning already is.

When you stop apologizing for it.

---

## The Meal

Every meal is a small act of authorship.

The plate. The pace. The order of things.

Whether it took six minutes or six hours, the way it is served and the way it is eaten will determine the way it is remembered.

---

A weeknight dinner —

served on a real plate,

eaten sitting down,

in fifteen unhurried minutes —

is not a more ambitious dinner.

It is the same dinner, treated with the seriousness it already deserves.

---

The question is rarely *what to eat.*

The question is *how to eat it.*

---

## The Table

The table is the most underestimated piece of furniture in the house.

It is a position, not an object.

A place where people are asked to sit down, set their phones aside, and stay a while.

---

To host is not to perform.

It is to extend, to one or to twelve, the way you already eat alone when you are eating well.

The candles do not impress anyone.

The pace does.

The willingness to take an hour does.

---

If you have a table, and you have used it once with care —

you already know how to host.

The rest is repetition.
