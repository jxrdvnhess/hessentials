There's a point where you stop tolerating bad coffee.

For most people, that never happens.

They stay with the same machine, the same pods, the same stale ground coffee.

It works.

But it's not good.

---

An espresso machine feels intimidating at first.

But after a few uses, it clicks.

And once it does, there's no going back.

---

You don't need to overcomplicate it.

A one-pound bag of beans, ground fine at the store, works.

It's fresher.

More flavorful.

Often cheaper than what people are already buying.

Store it airtight. Keep it cold. It holds.

---

Espresso isn't just stronger coffee.

It's cleaner.

More controlled.

More intentional.

You're not pressing a button.

You're making something.

---

## Iced coffee (this is where it really matters)

If you drink iced coffee, this is non-negotiable.

Espresso over ice is the only version that actually holds up.

No dilution.

No watered-down bitterness.

If you're me:

- at least 4 shots
- over ice
- two stevias
- a splash of whole milk

That's it.

---

Whole milk works because it balances the espresso instead of fighting it.

Simple.

---

Once you get used to making something better,  
you stop settling for the easier version.

---
